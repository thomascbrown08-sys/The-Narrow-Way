Day 13

Nothing Can Separate Us

And I am convinced that nothing can ever separate us from God's love. Neither death nor life, neither angels nor demons, neither our fears for today nor our worries about tomorrow—not even the powers of hell can separate us from God's love. No power in the sky above or in the earth below—indeed, nothing in all creation will ever be able to separate us from the love of God that is revealed in Christ Jesus our Lord. Romans 8:38–39 (NLT)

This is the summit of the whole letter, and very nearly the summit of the Bible's comfort — the place Paul has been climbing toward, and the place a pilgrim in Doubting Castle most needs to stand. Having spent thirteen days gathering promises against the dark, we arrive at the promise that gathers up all the rest: nothing can ever separate us from God's love.

Notice what Paul does, because it is exactly the right thing to do with our fears. He does not wave them away or pretend they are small. He names them, one after another, the whole catalog of what we are afraid might cut us off — death, life, angels, demons, our fears for today, our worries about tomorrow, the powers of hell, heights, depths — and against each one he sets the same verdict: cannot separate us. It is as if he invites every accusation into the courtroom precisely so that each can be answered and dismissed. He is not whistling in the dark. He has counted the dark, item by item, and found it powerless to do the one thing it threatens.

Do the same with your own list. Take the specific fear the giant has been using on you — the particular dread that whispers this will be the thing that finally separates you from God: this sin, this doubt, this failure, this grief, this long silence. Hold it up against the promise and watch where it lands. “Nothing in all creation” — and whatever your fear is, it is part of creation — “will ever be able to separate us from the love of God.” Your fear is real. Its power to separate you from God is not.

And see where Paul finally anchors the whole thing: “the love of God that is revealed in Christ Jesus our Lord.” This is not a vague optimism that things will work out. It is love with an address and a proof. How do you know the love will hold? Look at the cross. God has already done the hardest, costliest thing to keep you — he gave his own Son. A love that went that far to get you does not let go of you now over the very things Christ died to answer.

So let this be the ground you stand on when the dungeon is darkest. Not that you feel close to God — feelings come and go. But that nothing, in all of creation, has the power to separate you from a love that was proven at the cross and cannot be undone.

Pray the Promise

Make Paul's list your own. Name aloud the specific things you fear might separate you from God — and after each one, pray the promise: “...cannot separate me from your love.” Go through them one by one until the list is spent.

End where Paul ends, at the cross: “You proved this love by giving your Son. A love that went that far will not let me go now.”

For reflection

What is the one thing you most fear could cut you off from God? When you set it against “nothing in all creation,” and against the cross that proves the love, what happens to its power?
