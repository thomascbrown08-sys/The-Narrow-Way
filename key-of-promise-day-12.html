Day 12

Held in His Hand

I give them eternal life, and they will never perish. No one can snatch them away from me, for my Father has given them to me, and he is more powerful than anyone else. No one can snatch them from the Father's hand. John 10:28–29 (NLT)

Yesterday we heard that God will finish what he began. Today Jesus shows us the grip in which that finishing is secured — and he describes not one hand, but two. “No one can snatch them away from me,” he says, and then, as if to make doubly sure: “No one can snatch them from the Father's hand.” You are held in the hand of the Son, and around that, the hand of the Father. Two hands, infinite strength, closed around one trembling life.

Hear how absolute the promise is: “they will never perish. No one can snatch them away.” Not the devil. Not the world. Not your circumstances, however dark. Not the giant in his castle, for all his roaring. There is no power in heaven or earth strong enough to pry open the hands of God. Your security does not depend on your ability to hold on to him. It depends on his ability to hold on to you — and he is, as Jesus says plainly, “more powerful than anyone else.”

Now here is the question the despairing heart always asks, the one that feels like the exception that unravels the whole promise: but what about me? What if I am the one who lets go? The danger isn't out there — it's in here. I am the one I cannot trust. And it is worth seeing that this fear, real as it feels, has misunderstood the grip. You are not holding on to God by your own strength, one slip away from falling. You are held. A child carried in a strong father's arms is not kept safe by the tightness of the child's grip on the father. The child is kept by the father's grip on the child — and that grip does not loosen when the child grows weak, or frightened, or even when the child struggles.

So when the giant says, “You will let go, you will throw it all away, you are your own undoing,” answer him with the promise and let it be enough: no one can snatch me from his hand — and the ‘no one’ is wide enough to include even me at my worst. The hands that hold you are not waiting for you to fail. They are stronger than your failing.

There is rest in being held rather than holding. You may loosen your white-knuckled grip on your own salvation, not because the grip doesn't matter, but because it was never what kept you. What keeps you is the hand of God, and it will not open.

Pray the Promise

Name the fear that you yourself will let go — that you are the weak link — and pray the promise over it: “No one can snatch me from your hand. Not the enemy, not my circumstances. Not even me. You hold me; I do not hold myself.”

Picture the two hands — the Son's, and the Father's around it — and let yourself rest in being held rather than straining to hold.

For reflection

Is your sense of security based on your grip on God, or his grip on you? What fear is answered by knowing the “no one” who cannot snatch you away includes you at your weakest?
