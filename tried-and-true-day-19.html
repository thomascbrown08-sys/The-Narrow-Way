Day 19

What Does It Bear?

Beware of false prophets, who come to you in sheep's clothing but inwardly are ravenous wolves. You will recognize them by their fruits. Are grapes gathered from thornbushes, or figs from thistles? So, every healthy tree bears good fruit, but the diseased tree bears bad fruit. A healthy tree cannot bear bad fruit, nor can a diseased tree bear good fruit. Every tree that does not bear good fruit is cut down and thrown into the fire. Thus you will recognize them by their fruits. Matthew 7:15–20 (ESV)
But the fruit of the Spirit is love, joy, peace, patience, kindness, goodness, faithfulness, gentleness, self-control; against such things there is no law. Galatians 5:22–23 (ESV)

Jesus gives us his own criterion for evaluating claimed spiritual reality: fruit. Not immediate impressiveness, not the speaker's charisma, not the size of the response. Fruit — and specifically fruit assessed over time.

The fruit test requires patience. A thornbush can look like a grape vine before the growing season reveals what it actually is. False teachers often look compelling in the short term. It is the long view — what does this teaching produce in people's characters over years, not weeks? — that tells the truth.

Galatians 5's list is worth sitting with because it is notably unspectacular. Love. Joy. Peace. Patience. Kindness. Goodness. Faithfulness. Gentleness. Self-control. None of these are dramatic. None of them generate large crowds or impressive social media content. But they are what the Spirit actually produces when he is genuinely at work. If a teaching or experience is producing pride, division, dependency on a leader, emotional volatility, or drift from ordinary Christian virtue — those are the fruits of a different tree.

Apply this same test to yourself. What is your own spiritual life currently bearing? Not in terms of activity or feeling, but in terms of actual character formation?

Put It Into Practice

Think of a spiritual teaching or experience you've invested in — a church, a movement, a book, a practice. What fruit has it produced in your actual character over the past year? Be specific and honest.

For reflection

What would the fruit test reveal about your own spiritual life right now? Why does Jesus say 'you will recognize them by their fruits' rather than by their doctrine alone? What does fruit reveal that doctrine statements don't? How long should you observe before drawing conclusions from the fruit test? What patience does that require?
