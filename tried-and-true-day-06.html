Day 6

The Book That Measures Everything Else

All Scripture is breathed out by God and profitable for teaching, for reproof, for correction, and for training in righteousness, that the man of God may be complete, equipped for every good work. 2 Timothy 3:16–17 (ESV)
To the teaching and to the testimony! If they will not speak according to this word, it is because they have no dawn. Isaiah 8:20 (ESV)

Discernment needs a reference point. Without a fixed standard, we're simply measuring one opinion against another. The Reformers expressed this with the Latin phrase norma normans — the rule that rules all other rules. Scripture occupies that role.

This isn't a claim that the Bible answers every question directly. It doesn't tell you which job to take, or what to do about a specific medical decision. But it provides the criteria by which every significant question can be evaluated — what God is like, what human beings are, what we are for, what has gone wrong, what is being put right.

Isaiah's phrase is striking in its bluntness: if someone's teaching does not accord with this word, there is no dawn in them — no light. The Bible doesn't just claim to be one source among many. It claims to be the source that evaluates all others.

For discernment, this means something practical: whenever we are evaluating a teaching, an experience, or a claimed word from God, the first question is always what does Scripture say? Not as a shortcut that avoids thinking, but as the non-negotiable foundation that all further thinking rests on.

Put It Into Practice

Read one short passage of Scripture today — even five verses — slowly and carefully. Ask three questions: What does this tell me about God? What does it tell me about people? What does it tell me about how to live?

For reflection

How much do you actually trust the Bible as your reference point for evaluating truth? What competitors does it have in your life? What does it mean for Scripture to be 'sufficient'? Does it mean it answers every question, or something different? Can you think of a time when going back to Scripture changed what you believed or decided? What happened?
