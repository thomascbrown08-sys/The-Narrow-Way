Day 29

The Shape of a Discerning Person

And it is my prayer that your love may abound more and more, with knowledge and all discernment, so that you may approve what is excellent, and so be pure and blameless for the day of Christ. Philippians 1:9–10 (ESV)
And so, from the day we heard, we have not ceased to pray for you, asking that you may be filled with the knowledge of his will in all spiritual wisdom and understanding, so as to walk in a manner worthy of the Lord, fully pleasing to him: bearing fruit in every good work and increasing in the knowledge of God. Colossians 1:9–10 (ESV)

After thirty days thinking about what can go wrong and how to test it, it's worth pausing to paint the positive picture. What does a genuinely discerning person actually look like?

Paul's prayer in Philippians is telling: he prays for love that abounds more and more in knowledge and all discernment. He doesn't separate love from discernment. He treats them as deeply intertwined. The discerning person is not the suspicious one who keeps everyone at arm's length. It is the loving one whose love has been deepened and disciplined by knowledge of truth.

They are not primarily on guard. They are primarily on mission — walking worthy of the Lord, bearing fruit in every good work. Their discernment is not a fence around themselves; it is a tool in service of love for God and neighbor. They can recognize error because they know the real thing so well.

They are not joyless. The freedom that comes from a well-formed conscience and a trusted framework is genuine freedom. They don't have to examine everything with anxiety. They have a settled center, and from that center they can engage the world with curiosity rather than fear.

They are humble. They know they can be wrong. They hold their conclusions with appropriate firmness and appropriate tentativeness. They have counselors. They remain teachable.

Put It Into Practice

Name one person in your life who embodies discernment in the way described today. What specific habits or qualities do you observe in them? Write them down. These are things worth pursuing.

For reflection

What's the difference between the suspicious person and the discerning person? What does love have to do with it? What would it look like for you personally to grow in discernment over the next year? What's one specific step? How does humility function as a feature of discernment rather than a weakness in it?
