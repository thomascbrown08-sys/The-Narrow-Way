Day 18

The Undivided Heart

Teach me your ways, O LORD, that I may live according to your truth! Grant me purity of heart, so that I may honor you. Psalm 86:11 (NLT)
Wherever your treasure is, there the desires of your heart will also be. Your eye is like a lamp that provides light for your body. When your eye is healthy, your whole body is filled with light. Matthew 6:21–22 (NLT)

The phrase in the original Hebrew of Psalm 86:11 is remarkable: unite my heart. David is praying for an end to the internal division — the heart pulled in multiple directions at once, offering God something but not everything, worshiping while keeping something back.

We know this experience. We sit in church singing about surrender while inwardly protecting something we have no intention of releasing. We pray about trusting God with our future while the anxiety we won't name to him sits right behind our eyes. We bring most of ourselves. We hold something back.

Jesus' teaching about the lamp in Matthew 6 makes the same point from a different angle. The health of the eye determines whether the whole body has light. When our gaze is unified — when we are genuinely looking toward God rather than keeping one eye on something else — clarity follows. When our gaze is divided, everything is dim.

The prayer of Psalm 86:11 is one worth memorizing and returning to. It is honest about what we need — a heart that has been gathered from its scattered places and directed at the one thing. And it is wise enough to ask God for it rather than willing it into existence by determination alone.

Put It Into Practice

Pray Psalm 86:11 slowly and personally: "Teach me your ways, Lord, that I may live according to your truth. Unite my heart so that I may honor you." Let it be your honest request for today — and perhaps for much longer.

For reflection

What is currently dividing your heart — competing for the attention and allegiance that belongs to God? David asks God to unite his heart. That implies something active needs to happen — the scattered parts need to be gathered. Where are the scattered parts of your attention right now? What is the difference between having an undivided heart and simply feeling spiritually focused? Is it possible to have one without the other?
