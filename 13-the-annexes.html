The Annexes

A path off the heights, where honest pilgrims who love the same Lord have long disagreed.

"In essentials unity, in non-essentials liberty, in all things charity." a maxim long treasured by the church

Not every question on the road has a single answer that all faithful pilgrims have agreed upon. There is ground, just off the heights of the Delectable Mountains, where Christians who love the same Lord and read the same Scripture have walked to genuinely different conclusions — not because some love God less, but because the questions are hard and the Bible does not resolve them with the finality we might wish. This room is for that contested ground, and the rule of the house here is different from the rest of the road.

Everywhere else, this site teaches. Here, it maps. On these questions we will lay out the strongest case each side makes — the real concern each is protecting — before noting where the disagreement rests, so that you can navigate the terrain yourself rather than be handed a verdict. This is not because truth does not matter, but because humility about secondary questions is itself part of wisdom, and because a pilgrim is better served by understanding why thoughtful believers differ than by being told only one half exists.

In plain terms

Some questions are first-order — the deity of Christ, the gospel of grace — and on those the road does not equivocate. Others are real but secondary, where sincere Christians have always differed.

Telling the two apart is a skill worth having. On the secondary ones, you are allowed to hold your view firmly and still call the other side brothers.

Two annexes open off this path. The first is the long conversation between Calvinism and Arminianism — how God's sovereignty and human responsibility fit together in salvation. This site stands, openly, in the Reformed tradition; but we will give the Arminian his strongest voice before saying why we land where we do, because he is protecting something real, and because the shared ground beneath the dispute is larger than the dispute itself. The second is the question of science and faith — the two books, creation and Scripture, and how to read them without forcing a war between them that neither requires.

Everywhere else this site teaches. Here it maps — and lets you walk the ground yourself.
