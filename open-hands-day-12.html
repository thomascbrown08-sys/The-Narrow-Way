Day 12

Honesty in All Dealings

The LORD detests the use of dishonest scales, but he delights in accurate weights. Proverbs 16:11 (NLT) The LORD demands accurate scales and balances; he sets the standards for fairness. Leviticus 19:35–36 (NLT) "Do not use dishonest standards when measuring length, weight, or volume. Your scales and weights must be accurate. Your containers for measuring dry materials or liquids must be accurate. I am the LORD your God who brought you out of the land of Egypt." Proverbs 11:1 (NLT)

A "dishonest scale" in the ancient world was a rigged measuring instrument — one that looked legitimate but cheated the customer by recording less than was actually given, or more than was received. It was the fraud of the marketplace, commonplace enough that God addressed it directly, multiple times, in multiple books.

Two things are striking about these passages. First, the emotional language: God detests dishonest scales. This isn't mild disapproval. Business ethics aren't a peripheral concern that God tolerates as necessary for commerce — they are a matter of his character and his justice.

Second, notice the context in Leviticus 19. The command for accurate weights sits in the same chapter as "love your neighbor as yourself" (Leviticus 19:18). Honesty in transactions is a form of loving your neighbor. Cheating someone in a deal is a failure of love, not just a business problem.

The modern applications are obvious: contracts, reported hours, taxes, negotiations, any situation where accurate information matters and you control what's shared. God set the standard for fairness. He notices whether we meet it.
