Day 7

Enough for Every Test

Your word is a lamp to my feet and a light to my path. Psalm 119:105 (ESV)
His divine power has granted to us all things that pertain to life and godliness, through the knowledge of him who called us to his own glory and excellence. 2 Peter 1:3 (ESV)

A lamp on a dark path doesn't illuminate the entire landscape. It shows you the next step, and then the next. Psalm 119 doesn't promise that Scripture will answer every question you could ever ask. It promises that it will light the way as you walk.

Second Peter's claim is bolder: God's power has granted us everything that pertains to life and godliness. The Reformers called this the sufficiency of Scripture — not that the Bible contains all knowledge, but that it contains everything necessary for knowing God, living faithfully, and evaluating claims about both.

This is a liberating truth for discernment. We don't need to wait for a new revelation to answer the important questions. We don't need an extra layer of authoritative tradition on top of Scripture. We don't need a particularly gifted prophet to tell us what God really wants. Everything we need to evaluate spiritual claims is already given.

It is also a challenging truth. It places responsibility on us to actually know Scripture well enough to use it. A lamp in your hand only helps if you know how to hold it. The goal of the next several days is to think through what that looks like practically.

Put It Into Practice

Think of one difficult question you're currently wrestling with. Spend fifteen minutes searching Scripture for principles that might bear on it — not looking for a direct answer necessarily, but for the criteria that should shape your thinking.

For reflection

Is there a question where you've given up on Scripture being able to help? What might change if you looked more carefully? What's the difference between Scripture being 'sufficient' and Scripture being 'exhaustive'? What would you need to change about how you engage Scripture for it to function more effectively as a lamp in your life?
