Day 14

The Rare Value of True Friendship

A friend is always loyal, and a brother is born to help in time of need. Proverbs 18:24 (NLT) There are 'friends' who destroy each other, but a real friend sticks closer than a brother. Proverbs 27:17 (NLT) As iron sharpens iron, so a friend sharpens a friend. Proverbs 17:17 (NLT)

We move now from money to relationships — and we begin not with romance but with friendship. This is intentional. Proverbs does the same. A foundation of understanding what genuine friendship looks like makes everything that follows easier to navigate.

Proverbs 17:17 gives the diagnostic: a real friend is loyal always, and especially in adversity. The fair-weather friend — the one who is available in good times and disappears when things get hard — is a "friend" only in the casual sense. The test is what they do when you need them.

Proverbs 18:24 draws a striking contrast: many companions can actually produce ruin, but one real friend sticks closer than a brother. This is counterintuitive for those of us living in an era of social media, where friend-counts have become a metric. Breadth of connection doesn't substitute for depth. A thousand shallow relationships can leave you more isolated than one genuine one.

Proverbs 27:17 — iron sharpening iron — is one of the most widely quoted friendship verses, for good reason. Real friends don't just affirm you; they sharpen you. The friction of honest conversation, gentle challenge, and accountability is what makes friendship valuable beyond mere pleasantness. If your friends never challenge anything you think or do, they may not be sharpening you so much as reflecting you back.

Friendship is born at the moment when one person says to another, 'What! You too? I thought I was the only one.' — C.S. Lewis, The Four Loves
