Day 8

The Borrower Is Slave

Just as the rich rule the poor, so the borrower is servant to the lender. Romans 13:8 (NLT) Owe nothing to anyone — except for your obligation to love one another. If you love your neighbor, you will fulfill the requirements of God's law. Proverbs 22:7 (NLT)

The image in Proverbs 22:7 is stark: the borrower is a servant — the same word used for a slave. This is not hyperbole. Debt is a real constraint on freedom, and Proverbs wants us to feel the weight of it before we take it on lightly.

This doesn't mean all borrowing is sinful. Scripture doesn't prohibit mortgages or necessary loans. But it does insist that debt be treated seriously — as a form of obligation that limits your ability to respond freely to other claims on your resources.

Think practically: a person buried in consumer debt has less freedom to give generously, to take a lower-paying job that uses their gifts well, to respond to a need in a friend's life. The chains may be invisible, but they are real.

Romans 13:8 reframes the whole thing: the only debt Paul wants us to carry is love. That obligation we can never fully discharge — and it's the only one worth carrying indefinitely. Everything else, pay off. Get free.

For reflection

Is there any debt you're carrying that feels more like a chain than a tool? What would it take to address it?
