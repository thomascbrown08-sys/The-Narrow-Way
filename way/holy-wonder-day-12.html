Day 12

Spirit and Truth Together

But the time is coming — indeed it's here now — when true worshipers will worship the Father in spirit and in truth. The Father is looking for those who will worship him that way. For God is Spirit, so those who worship him must worship in spirit and in truth. John 4:23–24 (NLT)

Jesus is speaking to a Samaritan woman who has asked a theological question about the right place to worship. His answer bypasses the geography entirely and goes to what God is actually looking for: worship in spirit and in truth.

These two words are easily separated, and the separation in either direction is costly. Spirit without truth becomes pure emotionalism — worship shaped entirely by feeling, where the measure of a good service is whether we felt moved, whether something happened inside us, whether the music hit the right notes. This kind of worship is unstable because it depends on our emotional state rather than on who God actually is.

Truth without spirit becomes dry intellectualism — worship that is doctrinally correct, theologically informed, and utterly lifeless. The right words, the right confessions, the right liturgy — and no encounter with the living God. This kind of worship is safe but empty.

Spirit and truth together are the two wings that lift worship off the ground. The truth tells us who we are worshiping — the God of Abraham, the Father of Jesus, the holy fire. The spirit brings us genuinely into his presence rather than simply thinking about him from a distance. Both are necessary. Neither is sufficient alone.

Put It Into Practice

Assess your own worship honestly: do you lean toward spirit or truth? If you lean toward emotion, spend time this week reading a passage of Scripture about who God is before you begin to worship. If you lean toward truth, try worshiping through music or silence before you read.

For reflection

Which comes more naturally to you — the spirit side or the truth side of worship? What has shaped that tendency? What does it look like when someone has truth without spirit in their worship? What does it look like when they have spirit without truth? The Father is actively "looking for" true worshipers, Jesus says. What does it mean to you that God is seeking you out for this?
