Day 21

Get Counsel

Without wise leadership, a nation falls; there is safety in having many advisers. Proverbs 15:22 (NLT) Plans go wrong for lack of advice; many advisers bring success. Proverbs 12:15 (NLT) Fools think their own way is right, but the wise listen to others. Proverbs 11:14 (NLT)

One of our most reliable blindnesses is about ourselves. We tend to see our own motives as purer than they are, our own reasoning as clearer than it is, and our own desires as more obviously right than they actually are. This is not unique to you — it is the human condition. Proverbs is blunt about it.

Proverbs 12:15 doesn't say fools are intellectually inferior. It says fools are closed — convinced their way is right, not genuinely open to input. Wisdom is marked by an openness to correction and the active seeking of outside perspective. It is not weakness. It is accurate self-knowledge: I cannot see all the angles on my own situation, and I need people who know me and love me and will tell me the truth.

This is especially important in relationship decisions. The heart's capacity for self-deception is most powerful precisely when it wants something. A person can construct remarkably persuasive arguments for why this relationship is right even when the evidence points another way. The safeguard is people who know you well enough to see what you're not seeing.

The advisers Proverbs has in mind are not casual opinions from acquaintances. They are people with wisdom, people who know your character, people who have watched you over time — parents where possible, a pastor, older believers who will tell you the truth even when it's uncomfortable. Give those people permission to speak into the big decisions.

For reflection

Who in your life has real permission to speak hard truth into your relationship choices — and do they actually know enough about your life to do it?
