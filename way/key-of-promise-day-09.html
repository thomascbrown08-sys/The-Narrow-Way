Day 9

A New Heart, and My Spirit Within

And I will give you a new heart, and I will put a new spirit in you. I will take out your stony, stubborn heart and give you a tender, responsive heart. And I will put my Spirit in you so that you will follow my decrees and be careful to obey my regulations.

And now you Gentiles have also heard the truth, the Good News that God saves you. And when you believed in Christ, he identified you as his own by giving you the Holy Spirit, whom he had promised. The Spirit is God's guarantee that he will give us the inheritance he promised. Ezekiel 36:26–27; Ephesians 1:13–14 (NLT)

There is a despair deeper than guilt over what you have done. It is despair over what you are: “I cannot change. I have tried. My heart is cold and stubborn and it stays that way no matter what I do. Even my repentance doesn't last.” The giant loves this one, because it feels less like an accusation and more like a simple, hopeless fact.

So listen to what God promises — and notice that every verb belongs to him. “I will give you a new heart. I will put a new spirit in you. I will take out your stony heart. I will put my Spirit in you.” God does not stand over the stony heart commanding it to soften itself. He promises to do the surgery he is asking for. The change you have been straining to produce is a change he has promised to perform. This is grace reaching past your behavior all the way down to your nature — not just forgiving the cold heart, but replacing it.

And Ezekiel spoke this, again, to people who had every reason to think themselves beyond changing — exiles, dry bones, a nation that had failed for centuries. To them God promised not a final demand but a divine transplant. The hope was never that they would finally manufacture a tender heart. The hope was that God would give one.

Now hear how Paul says the promise has landed on you: “when you believed in Christ, he identified you as his own by giving you the Holy Spirit.” The Spirit Ezekiel promised has been given. He is in you. And Paul adds a word that speaks directly to the fear that you will never fully change: the Spirit is God's guarantee — the down-payment, the first installment that secures the rest. God has put a deposit on your transformation, and he does not forfeit deposits.

This means something quietly wonderful for the cold heart in the dungeon. The very fact that you grieve your coldness — that you want a warmer heart, that the stoniness bothers you at all — is itself evidence that the surgery has begun. A truly stony heart does not ache to be tender. The longing is the new heart, stirring. The Spirit is already within you, and he is the guarantee that the God who started this will finish it.

Pray the Promise

Stop straining to change your own heart tonight, and instead pray the promise that God will: “I cannot soften my own heart. But you have promised to give me a new one, and you have put your Spirit in me as the guarantee. Do in me what I cannot do in myself.”

Then take heart from the longing itself: the very desire to be different is the new heart stirring. Thank God for the ache as a sign of his work.

For reflection

Where have you been trying to manufacture a change of heart by willpower? What shifts if the new heart is something God promises to give, and your longing for it is already evidence of his Spirit at work?
