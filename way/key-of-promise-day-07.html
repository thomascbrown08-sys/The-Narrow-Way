Day 7

Near to the Brokenhearted

The Lord is close to the brokenhearted; he rescues those whose spirits are crushed.

The sacrifice you desire is a broken spirit. You will not reject a broken and repentant heart, O God. Psalm 34:18; 51:17 (NLT)

Doubting Castle does a cruel thing with your brokenness: it reads it as evidence against you. Look how crushed you are, the giant says. Look how little faith you have, how dry your prayers are, how heavy your heart is. Surely if God were near, you would not feel like this. Your very brokenness becomes, in his telling, the proof of God's distance.

Now hear what God says about the same brokenness, and feel how completely it reverses the verdict. “The Lord is close to the brokenhearted; he rescues those whose spirits are crushed.” The crushed spirit is not what drives God away. It is the very thing he draws near to. The broken heart is not what disqualifies you from his presence. It is, the psalmist dares to say, the “sacrifice” he desires — the offering he will never despise.

This is one of the great reversals of the gospel. We assume God is near the strong, the radiant, the spiritually impressive — and that brokenness is a waiting room we must pass through before he will come close. Scripture says the opposite. God is near the broken as broken. The nearness is not on the far side of your recovery. It is offered in the middle of the rubble.

Why would this be? Because a broken heart is a heart that has stopped pretending. The proud heart holds God at arm's length — it has its act together, needs nothing, keeps the relationship formal. The broken heart has run out of acts to put on. It is finally honest, finally empty, finally reachable. And God, who resists the proud, comes close to exactly such honesty. Your brokenness has not built a wall between you and God. It has, in a way you could not have engineered, opened the door.

So you do not need to assemble yourself into someone presentable before you approach. The brokenness is the approach. Bring him the crushed spirit. It is the one offering he has promised never to turn away.

Pray the Promise

Do not clean yourself up before you pray today. Bring God the brokenness exactly as it is, and pray the promise over it: “You are close to the brokenhearted. This crushed spirit is the very thing you have promised not to despise. So here it is. Come close.”

Let your prayer be the honest report of a broken heart, not the performance of a whole one.

For reflection

Have you been treating your brokenness as a barrier to God, something to fix before you come? What would it mean to bring it to him as the very thing he draws near to?
