Day 10

The Spirit Helps Us Pray

And the Holy Spirit helps us in our weakness. For example, we don't know what God wants us to pray for. But the Holy Spirit prays for us with groanings that cannot be expressed in words. And the Father who knows all hearts knows what the Spirit is saying, for the Spirit pleads for us believers in harmony with God's own will. Romans 8:26–27 (NLT)

Of all the cruelties of the dark, this may be the sharpest: that the very thing you most need to do — pray — becomes the thing you feel least able to do. The words won't come. You kneel and produce nothing but silence, or tears, or a groan that isn't even a sentence. And the giant leans in: you can't even pray properly. What kind of Christian can't pray?

Here is the promise that answers him, and it is almost too tender to believe. “The Holy Spirit helps us in our weakness... we don't know what God wants us to pray for. But the Holy Spirit prays for us with groanings that cannot be expressed in words.” Read it slowly. Your inability to find words is not the failure of prayer. It is named, right here in Scripture, as the ordinary condition of the praying Christian — “we don't know what to pray” — and into exactly that weakness the Spirit himself comes and prays for you, in groans too deep for words.

Think about what this means for the wordless prayer you managed tonight. That groan you thought was nothing — that sigh, that ache you couldn't articulate — was not lost. The Spirit took it up. He translated it. He carried it into the presence of the Father “in harmony with God's own will,” which means your fumbling, half-formed, barely-verbal prayer arrived before God refined into exactly the right petition. You are a worse pray-er than you fear and a better-represented one than you can imagine.

There is a beautiful symmetry hidden in this chapter. Earlier we heard that the Son intercedes for us at the Father's right hand. Here we hear that the Spirit intercedes for us from within our own hearts. You are prayed for from above and from within — the Son pleading in heaven, the Spirit groaning in you. Your prayer life, even at its most broken, is held inside the prayer of God for you.

So do not measure the worth of your prayers by their eloquence, or even their words. The weakest groan offered toward God is caught up by the Spirit and made perfect. On the nights you cannot pray, the Spirit is praying. You are not praying alone.

Pray the Promise

Tonight, let your groan be the prayer. Don't strain for words or worry that you're doing it wrong. Pray the promise simply: “I don't know how to pray right now. But you have promised that your Spirit prays for me with groans too deep for words. So I bring you my groan, and I trust you to carry it.”

Then offer God whatever you have — a word, a sigh, a silence — trusting that the Spirit is making it into the right prayer.

For reflection

Have you judged your prayers by how articulate they are? How does it change things to know that your wordless groans are taken up by the Spirit and carried perfectly to the Father?
