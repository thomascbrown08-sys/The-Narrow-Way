Day 30

Open Hands — Everything as Worship

And whatever you do or say, do it as a representative of the Lord Jesus, giving thanks through him to God the Father. Romans 11:33–36 (NLT) Oh, how great are God's riches and wisdom and knowledge! How impossible it is for us to understand his decisions and his ways!

For who can know the LORD's thoughts? Who knows enough to give him advice? And who has given him so much that he needs to pay it back?

For everything comes from him and exists by his power and is intended for his glory. All glory to him forever! Amen. Colossians 3:17 (NLT)

"Whatever you do or say." Not some things. Not the explicitly spiritual things. Whatever. How you handle your bank account. How you treat a friend. Whether you release a grudge or hold it. How you spend a Tuesday afternoon. Everything, in Paul's vision, is an opportunity to represent Jesus and offer thanks to the Father.

This is what we have been building toward for thirty days. Stewardship — open hands — is not a financial strategy or a relationship technique. It is a posture toward all of life: everything belongs to God, everything is entrusted to me for a season, and everything I do with it is either worship or something less.

Romans 11:33–36 is one of Paul's great doxologies — a moment where the argument becomes praise. He has spent eleven chapters laying out the theology of salvation and concludes not with a summary but with an exclamation: Oh, how great! He cannot help himself. The more clearly you see who God is and what he has done, the more naturally the response becomes worship rather than anxiety.

"For everything comes from him and exists by his power and is intended for his glory." This is the great circle of existence. Everything originates in God. Everything is sustained by God. Everything is moving back toward God. We are not outside this circle, managing our own affairs as autonomous agents. We are inside it, held, tended, and invited to participate.

Open hands. Not as a discipline of white-knuckle surrender, but as the natural posture of someone who has come to believe that the One who holds everything is trustworthy. May these thirty days be not the end of a study but the beginning of a practice.
