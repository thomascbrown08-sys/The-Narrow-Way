Day 30

Until We See His Face

Each of these living beings had six wings, and their wings were covered all over with eyes, inside and out. Day after day and night after night they keep on saying, "Holy, holy, holy is the Lord God, the Almighty — the one who always was, who is, and who is still to come." "You are worthy, O Lord our God, to receive glory and honor and power. For you created all things, and they exist because you created what you pleased." Revelation 4:8, 11 (NLT)
I heard a loud shout from the throne, saying, "Look, God's home is now among his people! He will live with them, and they will be his people. God himself will be with them. He will wipe every tear from their eyes, and there will be no more death or sorrow or crying or pain." Revelation 21:3–4 (NLT)

Everything we have practiced in these thirty days — the trembling, the drawing near, the lament, the praise, the ordinary moments offered up, the songs sung, the Table taken, the Sabbath kept — all of it is practice. Rehearsal for something coming.

Revelation shows us the destination: a throne room where the worship that has been rising since creation reaches its full sound. Where the living creatures who have never stopped crying "holy, holy, holy" are joined by countless thousands of every tribe and tongue and nation. Where the song that was being sung before the world began is finally sung in the presence of the One it has always been about.

And then: God's home is among his people. The distance between heaven and earth — between the holy fire and the creatures who needed coals pressed to their lips before they could come close — is abolished. He will be with them. Every tear wiped away. Every lament finally resolved. The one thing David sought — to behold the beauty of the LORD — becomes the permanent, unending reality.

This is what we are practicing for. Every Sunday gathering, every quiet morning of prayer, every distracted and honest and imperfect attempt at worship is pointing toward that room. We are not there yet. But we are on the way. And the God who set us on this path will be the one who meets us at its end.

Put It Into Practice

Read Revelation 4–5 today — the full throne room scene. Let it be the vision you carry forward: not of worship as obligation, but of worship as homecoming. Then write one sentence about what you want to take from these thirty days into the rest of your life.

For reflection

How does knowing that all worship points toward Revelation 4–5 change how you approach the worship you practice today? Augustine says our hearts are restless until they rest in God. Where do you feel that restlessness most in your life right now? What is the one thing from these thirty days that you most want to carry forward? What practice, posture, or truth has taken root in you?
