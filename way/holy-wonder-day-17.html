Day 17

The Hazard of Habit

And so the Lord says, "These people say they are mine. They honor me with their lips, but their hearts are far from me. And their worship of me is nothing but man-made rules learned by rote." Isaiah 29:13 (NLT)
"These people honor me with their lips, but their hearts are far from me. Their worship is a farce, for they teach man-made ideas as commands from God." Matthew 15:8–9 (NLT)

Jesus quotes Isaiah 29 in Matthew 15 — and the fact that the same diagnosis applies six centuries apart should give us pause. This is not an ancient problem. It is the default drift of human religious life.

The people God is describing through Isaiah are not atheists. They are not pagans who have abandoned him for other gods. They say these people are mine. They go through the forms. They speak the right words. And their hearts are somewhere else entirely.

Habit is a gift when it carries us through seasons when feeling is absent. The person who has learned to pray in the morning even when they don't feel like it is wiser than the one who only prays when moved. Routine creates structure that sustains the life of faith.

But habit has a shadow side: it can take over completely, running the routines while the heart quietly checks out. We can go to church, sing the songs, take the communion, and never once actually encounter God. Our lips move. Our minds are elsewhere. God notices. He is not interested in a relationship with our habits. He wants us.

Put It Into Practice

Pick one worship habit you have — daily prayer, Sunday church, grace before meals — and interrupt it this week. Not to abandon it, but to do it differently enough that it requires your actual attention. Pray aloud instead of silently. Sit somewhere different in church. Let the interruption wake you up.

For reflection

Where has habit become a substitute for genuine engagement in your worship life? How long has it been that way? What is the difference between faithful routine and empty routine? How do you tell them apart from the inside? God says their hearts are "far from" him while their mouths say otherwise. How far would you honestly place your heart from God right now?
