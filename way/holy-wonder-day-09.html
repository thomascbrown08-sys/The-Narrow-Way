Day 9

Fear as a Gift

And I will make an everlasting covenant with them: I will never stop doing good for them. I will put a desire in their hearts to worship me, and they will never leave me. Jeremiah 32:40 (NLT)
Fear of the LORD is the foundation of wisdom. Knowledge of the Holy One results in good judgment. Proverbs 9:10 (NLT)

Here is something we are not told often enough: the fear of the LORD is not something you manufacture by trying harder. It is something God gives. Jeremiah 32:40 says it plainly — God will put the desire to worship in their hearts. He will instill it. He will be the one who produces in us the reverence that opens the door to everything else.

This is both humbling and relieving. Humbling because it means we cannot generate genuine worship through spiritual discipline alone. You can go to church every Sunday and never once truly worship. You can pray every morning and never once actually encounter God. The capacity for genuine reverence is itself a gift of grace.

Relieving because it means the answer to flat, dry, distracted worship is not simply to try harder. It is to ask. To come to God and say honestly: I don't fear you the way I want to. I don't love you the way you deserve. Give me what I lack. That prayer is one God delights to answer.

Proverbs calls the fear of the LORD the foundation of wisdom — not one component among others, but the ground everything else rests on. When this is alive in us, discernment clarifies, relationships deepen, priorities reorder, and life begins to make a different kind of sense. The fear of God is not a single virtue we add to our list. It is the soil in which all the other virtues grow.

Put It Into Practice

Pray this prayer simply and honestly today: "Lord, I want to fear you rightly. I want to see you clearly enough that reverence and love grow in me together. Give me what I cannot manufacture myself." Then hold that request open throughout your day.

For reflection

Have you ever asked God to give you a deeper fear and reverence for him? What happened, or what has kept you from asking? How does knowing that the fear of the LORD is a gift change your posture toward developing it? Proverbs says the fear of the LORD is the foundation of wisdom. Where in your life do you most need that foundation to be stronger?
