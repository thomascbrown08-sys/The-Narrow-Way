Tried and True

Tried and True

A 30-day study in spiritual discernment — testing teaching, weighing decisions, and evaluating the heart, in the shepherds' country.

"...solid food is for the mature, for those who have their powers of discernment trained by constant practice to distinguish good from evil." Hebrews 5:14 (ESV)

Among the heights of the Delectable Mountains, the shepherds do for the pilgrims one particular service again and again: they teach them to see. They walk them to Mount Error to show them the cliff where the deceived fall, and to Mount Caution to show them those who strayed and were taken. This companion is the shepherds' lesson turned into a daily practice. It is a thirty-day study in spiritual discernment — learning to distinguish what is real, what is right, and what is from God, in teaching, in decisions, and in the quiet impressions of your own heart.

It belongs to this estate because discernment is exactly what the high country is for. The bestiary of Mount Error, the charitable weighing of the Annexes, the sober warning of Mount Caution — all of it is the same trained capacity this study aims to build. And the study is clear, from its first day to its last, about what discernment is and is not: not a superpower, not suspicion, not heresy-hunting, but a slowly-trained skill — and one whose end, as the final day insists, is not to make a critic but a lover of the truth.

How to use these thirty days

Take one day at a time, in order, or begin wherever you are. Each day gives you a short passage of Scripture, a few paragraphs to help it land, a way to put it into practice, and a question to sit with. The aim is not to become quick to judge, but slow, careful, and clear — the way the shepherds were.

Discernment is not a superpower. It is a trained capacity — and its end is not a heresy-hunter, but a lover of what is true.
