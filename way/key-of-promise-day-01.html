Day 1

I Will Be Your God

I will confirm my covenant with you and your descendants after you, from generation to generation. This is the everlasting covenant: I will always be your God and the God of your descendants after you. Genesis 17:7 (NLT)

Begin here, in the dark, with the most astonishing sentence God ever spoke about himself: I will always be your God. Not “I might be, if you prove yourself.” Not “I will be, as long as you keep up.” Just — I will. The infinite God, who needs nothing and no one, who was perfectly happy in himself before the world began, binds himself by an oath to a people. He makes himself theirs.

Notice who does the binding. Abraham does not climb up to God and negotiate terms. God comes down to Abraham and announces them. The covenant is God's initiative from first to last — his promise, his oath, his everlasting word. And that is precisely the thing a despairing heart most needs to hear, because despair always frames the relationship the wrong way round. Despair says: the question is whether you have done enough to keep God. The covenant says: the question was settled before you were born, by God's decision to be yours.

This is why Christian, locked in the dungeon, eventually remembered that the key was in his own bosom all along. He had not earned it that morning. He had not felt worthy of it. It was simply his, given, carried, waiting to be used. The promises of God are like that key. They do not become true when you feel them. They are true because the One who cannot lie has spoken them, and he has made them yours.

Giant Despair's whole strategy is to make you doubt that you belong to God at all — to make the covenant feel like a contract you have already broken. But a contract depends on both parties keeping their word. A covenant of grace depends on the One who swore it. “I will be your God” does not rest on your grip. It rests on his oath. And his oath does not waver when your feelings do.

So before we say anything else in these days about the dark, say this first and slowly: the God of the whole universe has spoken your relationship into being and called it everlasting. You did not start it. You cannot finish it. He will be your God.

Pray the Promise

Take the promise and pray it back to God, out loud if you can, even — especially — if you do not feel it. Say: “You have said you will be my God. I did not earn this and I cannot break it. I hold you to your own word.”

Praying a promise is not pretending to feel something you don't. It is doing what Christian did: taking the key you already hold and turning it in the lock while it is still dark.

For reflection

Where have you been quietly believing that staying in God's family depends on your performance? What changes if the covenant was his idea, sworn by his oath, before you ever responded?
