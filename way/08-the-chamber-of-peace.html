The Chamber of Peace

The bedchamber called Peace, whose window opens toward the sunrise.

"Therefore, since we have been justified by faith, we have peace with God through our Lord Jesus Christ." Romans 5:1 (ESV)

When Christian was received into Palace Beautiful, he was given a room to sleep in. Bunyan tells us its name: the chamber was called Peace, and its window opened toward the sunrise. He woke in it singing. After the climb of the Hill, after the long road from the City, the pilgrim was given a place simply to rest — and to wake facing the dawn.

This is the room for assurance and rest, and it is rightly placed inside the church, because it is in the company of God's people, under the Word and at the table, that a weary pilgrim most often recovers the settled peace that the road wears thin. We have spoken of assurance already — the sealed scroll at the Cross, the scroll lost and found on the Hill. Here we simply rest in it.

The peace on offer is not the absence of trouble, and it is not a mood you must manufacture by trying harder to feel calm. It is the settledness of a person who has stopped depending on the strength of their own grip and has learned to depend on the strength of God's. Therefore, since we have been justified by faith, we have peace with God — not a truce we maintain by good behavior, but a peace already secured, into which we are invited to lie down and sleep.

In plain terms

Christian peace is not "nothing is wrong." It is "I am held, whatever is wrong." The window of this room faces the sunrise because the morning is coming regardless of how dark the night has felt.

You are allowed to rest here. The pilgrim who never lies down does not last the journey.

Assurance does not look inward at our grip on Christ. It looks to the Cross at his grip on us.

We will say more, in time, about how a soul recovers and keeps this peace — the means God uses, the rhythms of grace, what to do when the peace goes missing. For now it is enough to have a room with a name like Peace, and a window that faces the light. Christian slept, and woke singing, and went on.
