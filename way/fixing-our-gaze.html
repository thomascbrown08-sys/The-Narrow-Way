Fixing Our Gaze

A 90-day devotional whose single purpose is to behold the person of Jesus — the companion for the whole road, returning the pilgrim again and again to the steady gaze that finishes the race.

"...let us run with endurance the race that is set before us, looking to Jesus, the founder and perfecter of our faith." Hebrews 12:1–2 (ESV)

Every other study on this site belongs to a place — a landmark, a room, a peak. This one belongs to the whole road. Fixing Our Gaze is the companion meant to walk beside a pilgrim through every country of the journey, from the City of Destruction to the gates of the Celestial City, because it does the one thing every stretch of the road requires equally: it looks at Jesus. The writer of Hebrews gave the image — let us run with endurance the race set before us, fixing our eyes on Jesus — and named the secret of finishing: not staring at the road, not measuring our own progress, but keeping our eyes on the One who runs ahead and waits at the end.

It is a ninety-day journey, the longest of all the companions, and its purpose is the simplest. Not to accumulate more doctrines about Christ, though doctrine has its rightful and necessary place; not to marshal more arguments on his behalf, though the road has rooms for those too. Its single, repeated aim is to behold the Person himself — to look, day after day, at who Jesus is, until the looking begins to change the one who looks. The ninety days trace a loose arc, from the eternal glory of the Son, through his incarnate ministry, to his death, resurrection, reign, and our coming hope — but each day is also a single facet of the diamond, and can be read alone, in whatever sequence life provides.

How each day is arranged

Each day opens with a short prayer — a posture, not a formality — then a single Scripture passage drawn from across the Bible in a variety of trusted translations. A meditation draws on the riches of Christian history — Spurgeon, Owen, Bavinck, Bonhoeffer, Stott, Keller, and others, not as authorities over Scripture but as fellow gazers. Reflection questions move you from reading to responding, and an optional Going Deeper note grounds the doctrine beneath the day.

We become like what we look at. The arguments clear the way and the doctrines guard the truth — but it is the looking that remakes the soul.
