Day 4

Obedience as Worship

And so, dear brothers and sisters, I plead with you to give your bodies to God because of all he has done for you. Let them be a living and holy sacrifice — the kind he will find acceptable. This is truly the way to worship him. Don't copy the behavior and customs of this world, but let God transform you into a new person by changing the way you think. Then you will learn to know God's will for you, which is good and pleasing and perfect. Proverbs 3:9–10 (NLT) Honor the LORD with your wealth and with the best part of everything you produce. Then he will fill your barns with grain, and your vats will overflow with good wine. Romans 12:1–2 (NLT)

"A living and holy sacrifice." The phrase is deliberately paradoxical — a sacrifice is something killed and placed on an altar. Paul says: stay alive, but give yourself the same way.

This is one of the most important verses in the whole New Testament for understanding what the Christian life actually is. Worship is not something that happens on Sunday morning for an hour. Worship is the orientation of an entire life toward God. The way you handle your bank account is worship — or its absence. How you treat the person you're hoping to date is worship. Whether you hold onto a grudge or release it is worship.

Proverbs 3:9 lands this concretely. "Honor the LORD with your wealth and with the best part of everything you produce." Not the leftovers. The best part, the first part — offered as an act of recognition that it all came from him. The "firstfruits" principle runs throughout Scripture: give from the top, not from what remains after everything else is covered.

Paul adds a crucial second note in Romans 12:2: don't be conformed to this world's pattern of thinking. Our culture has very particular ideas about money and relationships and what we owe each other — many of which quietly contradict what God says. Being transformed requires noticing the gap.

Worship is not primarily an activity we perform but a posture we inhabit. The daily life of the Christian, offered to God, is itself an act of praise. — J.I. Packer, Knowing God

For reflection

In what area of life do you most tend to separate 'worship' from daily decisions?
