Day 30

Not a Heresy Hunter — A Lover

If I speak in the tongues of men and of angels, but have not love, I am a noisy gong or a clanging cymbal. And if I have prophetic powers, and understand all mysteries and all knowledge, and if I have all faith, so as to remove mountains, but have not love, I am nothing. 1 Corinthians 13:1–2 (ESV)
Finally, brothers, whatever is true, whatever is honorable, whatever is just, whatever is pure, whatever is lovely, whatever is commendable, if there is any excellence, if there is anything worthy of praise, think about these things. Philippians 4:8 (ESV)

We end where we should: with love and with beauty. Because that is where the whole enterprise of discernment actually begins and ends.

Discernment is not primarily a defensive posture. It is not fundamentally about what to avoid, what to reject, or who to distrust. It is rooted in love — love for God who is true, love for people who deserve truth rather than comfortable error, and love for the beauty of the gospel that is worth protecting.

Paul's last instruction in Philippians 4:8 is to direct our minds toward what is true, honorable, just, pure, lovely, commendable, excellent, worthy of praise. This is the positive heart of discernment: a mind trained not merely to recognize what is false, but to dwell on what is genuinely good. The person who knows beauty deeply is harder to deceive with counterfeits. The person who loves the real thing is not fooled by imitations.

You began this study somewhere. You asked questions, sat with uncertainty, maybe pushed back on things that felt uncomfortable. That is exactly right. The life of discernment is not a final arrival — it is a long, patient walk with a lamp in hand, one step illuminated at a time.

Keep walking. Keep examining. Keep loving. And keep your eyes on Christ — who is himself the truth, and the only one in whom discernment finally rests.

Put It Into Practice

Write down three things that are true, one thing that is honorable, and one thing that is lovely — as Philippians 4:8 invites. Spend ten minutes dwelling on them today. Let this be how you finish.

For reflection

How has your understanding of discernment changed through these thirty days? What do you most want to carry forward? What is the difference between a discerning person and a suspicious one? Now that you've spent thirty days on this, how would you answer that differently than when you started? Discernment grows through constant practice. What is one habit you will commit to that will keep growing your discernment long after this study ends?
