Day 17

Testing What You Feel

Do not quench the Spirit. Do not despise prophecies, but test everything; hold fast what is good. Abstain from every form of evil. 1 Thessalonians 5:19–22 (ESV)
When my soul was embittered, when I was pricked in heart, I was brutish and ignorant; I was like a beast toward you. Psalm 73:21–22 (ESV)

Interior experience — feelings, impressions, deep convictions, senses of peace or unease — is real and matters. The Christian life is not lived entirely in the head. Dismissing interior experience wholesale is both unbiblical and dishonest.

But interior experience must be tested. First Thessalonians gives us a balanced command: do not quench the Spirit, do not despise prophecies — and test everything. The openness and the testing belong together. Neither replaces the other.

The Psalmist in Psalm 73 models what untested interior experience can produce. He was bitter, embittered — and he interpreted his bitterness as a spiritual insight about the unfairness of the world. It took an encounter with God's perspective (Psalm 73:16–17) for him to see that his emotional state had been distorting his perception of reality. He calls himself 'brutish and ignorant.' The feeling was real. The interpretation was wrong.

The key question when examining interior experience is not how strong is this feeling? but where is this feeling taking me? Strong feelings are not evidence of spiritual reality — grief, joy, certainty, and peace can all be generated by things other than the Holy Spirit. The question is whether the experience is orienting you toward God and neighbor, or toward something else.

Put It Into Practice

Think of a strong feeling or impression you've had recently in a spiritual context. Ask: where did this feeling lead me? Did it increase love for God and neighbor, or did it increase focus on myself, my status, or my experience?

For reflection

How much weight do you currently give to strong feelings or impressions in your spiritual life? What standard do you use to evaluate them? Can you think of a time when an intense spiritual feeling led you somewhere you later questioned? What did you learn? What's the difference between testing your feelings and dismissing them?
