Day 20

Becoming, Not Just Waiting

"If you are faithful in little things, you will be faithful in large ones. But if you are dishonest in little things, you won't be honest with greater responsibilities." Proverbs 18:22 (NLT) The man who finds a wife finds a treasure, and he receives favor from the LORD. Matthew 25:21 (NLT) "The master said, 'Well done, my good and faithful servant. You have been faithful in handling this small amount, so now I will give you many more responsibilities. Let's celebrate together!'" Luke 16:10 (NLT)

There is a passive assumption that quietly shapes many believers' approach to finding a spouse: God will bring the right person at the right time, so the primary task is simply to wait. This is half true. God does provide. But the picture Scripture gives is less like sitting in a room waiting for a package and more like a servant faithfully tending to everything entrusted to them — and finding that the master, upon returning, gives more.

Luke 16:10 applies directly: faithfulness in small things is both preparation for and demonstration of readiness for larger responsibilities. The character habits you build now — how you handle money, how you treat friends, whether you keep your word, whether you pursue holiness — are not separate from your readiness for a lifelong covenant. They are the very substance of it.

Proverbs 18:22 is worth noticing: "the man who finds a wife." The verb is active. Finding requires looking, moving, engaging with life in communities where growth happens. This doesn't mean manufacturing situations or trying to accelerate what God has not yet opened. It means being a person out in the world, growing, serving, investing — someone who can be found as well as find.

The point is not "work harder to earn a spouse." It is simpler and deeper than that: the person who faithfully tends what they've been given is already living the life of a good steward. Everything good — including the relationships God brings — tends to grow in that soil.

For reflection

What is one concrete area of your character or habits where you could grow in faithfulness this week — not to attract someone, but because it's the right way to live?
