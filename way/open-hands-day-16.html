Day 16

The Body Is a Temple

Run from sexual sin! No other sin so clearly affects the body as this one does. For sexual immorality is a sin against your own body. Don't you realize that your body is the temple of the Holy Spirit, who lives in you and was given to you by God? You do not belong to yourself, for God bought you with a high price. So you must honor God with your body. 1 Thessalonians 4:3–5 (NLT) God's will is for you to be holy, so stay away from all sexual sin. Then each of you will control his own body and live in holiness and honor — not in lustful passion like the people who don't know God and his ways. 1 Corinthians 6:18–20 (NLT)

This is not an easy topic, and it shouldn't be approached with shame as the primary instrument. The goal of these passages isn't to make you feel guilty about your body or your desires — God designed sexual desire, and it is not inherently disordered. The question is the context for its expression.

Paul's argument in 1 Corinthians 6 is remarkable. He doesn't say "sexual sin is embarrassing" or "it's socially inappropriate." He says it's uniquely intimate — a sin against your own body in a way other sins are not. The reason this matters is that your body isn't simply yours. It is the dwelling place of the Holy Spirit. It belongs to God. That changes everything about how it is to be treated — both by you and by others.

"God bought you with a high price" — the language of purchase here refers to the cross. Christ's death is the cost of our redemption, and that redemption includes the body, not just the soul. A high-priced thing is handled with care.

The contrast in 1 Thessalonians 4:5 is striking: "not like people who don't know God." The implication is that knowing God — actually knowing him, not just knowing about him — changes how we relate to our own desires. Chastity isn't a rule that falls from the sky; it flows from a relationship.

For reflection

How does thinking of your body as a temple — something indwelt by God himself — change how you think about your physical life?
