Day 17

Flee and Pursue

Run from anything that stimulates youthful lusts. Instead, pursue righteous living, faithfulness, love, and peace. Enjoy the companionship of those who call on the Lord with pure hearts. Proverbs 4:23 (NLT) Guard your heart above all else, for it determines the course of your life. Song of Songs 2:7 (NLT) Promise me, O women of Jerusalem, by the gazelles and wild deer, not to awaken love until the time is right. 2 Timothy 2:22 (NLT)

There are two verbs in 2 Timothy 2:22, and both matter: run from and pursue. Chastity is not simply the absence of sin. It is the active pursuit of something better. If all you're doing is trying not to fall, you have only one strategy and it requires constant effort. If you are actively running toward righteousness, faithfulness, love, and peace, you have a destination — and that changes everything.

Proverbs 4:23 is one of the most important verses in the whole book: "Guard your heart above all else, for it determines the course of your life." The heart here is not primarily emotions — it is the seat of will, desire, and decision. What you allow to take root there shapes the direction of everything else. Guarding your heart is not the same as closing it off; it means being intentional about what you feed it, what you dwell on, what you give access to.

Song of Songs 2:7 is often overlooked, but it contains a kind of wisdom that is rare in discussions of sexuality: "do not awaken love until the time is right." This is not a statement about love being dangerous or shameful. It is wisdom about timing and readiness. Some good things, opened before their time, become sources of pain rather than joy. Waiting is not repression — it is the recognition that the right thing in the wrong season can still cost you.

We are half-hearted creatures, fooling about with drink and sex and ambition when infinite joy is offered us, like an ignorant child who wants to go on making mud pies in a slum because he cannot imagine what is meant by the offer of a holiday at the sea. — C.S. Lewis, The Weight of Glory

For reflection

What does 'pursuing righteousness' look like practically in your daily life — not just avoiding wrong, but actively pursuing something better?
