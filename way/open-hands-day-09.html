Day 9

Saving and Planning Ahead

The wise have wealth and luxury, but fools spend whatever they get. Proverbs 13:11 (NLT) Wealth from get-rich-quick schemes quickly disappears; wealth from hard work grows over time. Proverbs 27:23–24 (NLT) Know the state of your flocks, and put your heart into caring for your herds, for riches don't last forever, and the crown might not be passed to the next generation. Proverbs 21:20 (NLT)

Proverbs has no romantic illusions about wealth — it doesn't promise riches to the faithful, and it doesn't pretend that money isn't real. Instead, it consistently praises the wisdom of building slowly, spending carefully, and knowing your financial situation clearly.

Proverbs 21:20 is almost funny in its plainness: the wise have something left; fools spend it all. There's no complicated secret here. Spending less than you earn — consistently, over time — is wisdom. It isn't glamorous, but it is what Proverbs calls it: wealth and luxury, eventually.

The warning in 13:11 is as relevant today as ever. Get-rich-quick schemes, high-risk shortcuts, financial windfalls that require no work — these don't build. Slow, steady, unglamorous accumulation does. The contrast isn't between risk and safety; it's between patient faithfulness and impatient grasping.

Proverbs 27:23 uses a farming image: "know the state of your flocks." In plain terms: know where you actually stand financially. Many people avoid looking at their bank accounts or budgets because the knowledge is uncomfortable. But you cannot steward what you refuse to see. Knowing the truth of your situation — even an uncomfortable truth — is the beginning of wisdom, not a source of shame.
