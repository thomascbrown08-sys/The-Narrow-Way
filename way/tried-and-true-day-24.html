Day 24

Four Errors Worth Knowing

Beware of false prophets, who come to you in sheep's clothing but inwardly are ravenous wolves. Matthew 7:15 (ESV)
Not that there is another one, but there are some who trouble you and want to distort the gospel of Christ. Galatians 1:7 (ESV)

Four major errors circulate widely in Western Christianity today. Knowing their shape makes them recognizable. Each begins with something true and takes it somewhere wrong.

Moralistic Therapeutic Deism is the dominant popular religion of our moment. It holds that God exists, wants us to be good and happy, is available in times of need, and doesn't otherwise make many demands. It gets the existence of God right, and the importance of morality, and the availability of divine comfort. It removes sin, the cross, repentance, and the claim that God has any specific authority over human life.

The Prosperity Gospel rightly affirms God's goodness and desire to bless his people. It distorts this into the claim that faith produces health and wealth, and that suffering is evidence of insufficient faith. It makes God a cosmic vending machine and turns suffering into a spiritual failure.

Progressive Christianity's drift often begins with genuine pastoral concern for those harmed by rigid or graceless versions of the faith. It ends by revising or abandoning core doctrines — the authority of Scripture, the exclusivity of Christ, the reality of judgment — in response to cultural pressure rather than biblical argument.

New Age syncretism in Christian clothing blends Christian vocabulary with practices and beliefs from other traditions — energy work, spirit guides, manifesting, ancestor communication — without recognizing that these are not neutral practices but are addressed directly by Scripture.

Put It Into Practice

Of these four errors, which are you most likely to encounter in your context? Which do you find most appealing — and therefore most dangerous to you personally? Be honest.

For reflection

What true thing does each of these errors take and distort? Why does the partial truth make the distortion harder to see? Why is pastoral concern (as in progressive drift) not sufficient grounds for revising core doctrine? Which of these errors have you personally encountered? How did you recognize it — or fail to?
