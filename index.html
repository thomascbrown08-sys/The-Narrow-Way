The Narrow Way

Thinking Carefully About Christian Faith
For the gate is narrow and the way is hard that leads to life, and those who find it are few. Matthew 7:14

This is one road, not a shelf of separate books. Like the pilgrim in Bunyan's old story, you are already somewhere on it — and every landmark here is a place on the same way, from the city we leave to the city we are bound for. Begin where you actually stand.

Start Here

Which describes where you are?

You don't have to start at the beginning. You have to start where you are. Choose the door that fits, and it will set you down at the right place on the road.

New here · or wrestling with doubt

I'm not sure what I believe yet.

Start at the gate, where the hard questions are welcome: is any of this true, and can an honest mind walk in without checking its brain at the door?

Wounded by the church

The people who should have been safe weren't.

You are not a lesser case or an edge case. There is ground on this road set apart for the hurt — and the God described there is the one who reaches down into the mire.

The Map of the Way

The road, in the order it is walked

Bunyan gave each stretch of the pilgrim's road a name. Each one is a place to stop and learn something the journey requires. The old name sits on top; the plain subject is underneath, so you are never lost even if you have never read The Pilgrim's Progress.

A Companion for the Whole Way
