Day 2

I Will Never Leave You

So be strong and courageous! Do not be afraid and do not panic before them. For the Lord your God will personally go ahead of you. He will neither fail you nor abandon you.

Do not be afraid or discouraged, for the Lord will personally go ahead of you. He will be with you; he will neither fail you nor abandon you. Deuteronomy 31:6, 8 (NLT)

There is a particular kind of darkness that is worse than trouble: the darkness of feeling alone in the trouble. Not just “this is hard” but “this is hard and God is nowhere.” The heavens feel like brass. Prayers seem to stop at the ceiling. And the silence gets read as a verdict: he has left.

Hear, then, exactly what God promised — and exactly what he did not. He did not promise, “You will always feel me near.” He promised, “I will neither fail you nor abandon you.” Those are very different sentences. The first is about your experience, which rises and falls with sleep and weather and grief. The second is about his commitment, which does not move at all. Felt nearness is a gift God gives sometimes. Actual nearness is a fact he guarantees always.

This matters enormously in a place like Doubting Castle, because the giant's favorite lie is to take an absence of feeling and sell it to you as an absence of God. But your feelings are not instruments precise enough to measure the presence of God. A submarine does not vanish because the sailors inside cannot see the ocean. God does not leave because you cannot feel him.

And here is the deepest ground of the promise. The reason God can swear never to abandon you is that, at the cross, he abandoned his own Son in your place. “My God, my God, why have you forsaken me?” — that cry was wrung out of Jesus so that it would never have to be true of you. The forsakenness you fear was absorbed by him. He was left in the dark so that you never will be.

So when the silence comes, do not trust the silence. Trust the promise that speaks into it. He has gone ahead of you. He is with you. He will neither fail you nor abandon you — not because you feel it tonight, but because he said it, and because his Son made it sure.

Pray the Promise

Name the silence honestly to God rather than pretending it isn't there. Then pray the promise straight into it: “I cannot feel you right now. But you have said you will never leave me, and I trust your word over my feelings.”

If even that is hard to say, simply repeat the last line of the promise as your whole prayer: he will neither fail me nor abandon me.

For reflection

When God feels absent, what do you tend to conclude — about him, and about yourself? How would today change if felt-distance and actual-distance were genuinely two different things?
